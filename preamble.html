The Preamble of the Indian Constitution

The Preamble of the Constitution of India is a brief introductory statement that sets forth the guiding principles and core values of the Constitution. It serves as the foundation for the Constitution and provides an insight into the aspirations of the Indian people. The Preamble reads:

"We, the people of India, having solemnly resolved to constitute India into a Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, Democratic Republic and to secure to all its citizens:

Justice, social, economic, and political; Liberty of thought, expression, belief, faith, and worship; Equality of status and of opportunity; and to promote among them all Fraternity assuring the dignity of the individual and the unity and integrity of the Nation."

Sovereign

India is a sovereign state, meaning it is free to make its own laws and is not subject to any external authority.

Socialist

The term 'socialist' reflects the aim of achieving social and economic justice for all, ensuring equitable distribution of wealth.

Secular

India does not favor any particular religion and grants equal freedom to all religions.

Democratic

India is a democracy, where the people elect their leaders through free and fair elections.

Republic

India has an elected head of state (the President) instead of a monarch, ensuring a republican form of governance.
