Principle 6
Promotion of Welfare of People

This principle encourages the state to work towards improving the welfare of the people, especially the underprivileged.

Example:

The Right to Food Act ensures subsidized food to the underprivileged population.

Social and Economic Justice

The government should reduce inequality and provide equal opportunities.

Example:

Reservation policies for backward communities in education and employment.

Right to Adequate Livelihood

Every citizen should have the right to work and earn a decent living.

Example:

MGNREGA, which provides rural employment opportunities.

Promotion of Education

The state must ensure free and compulsory education for children.

Example:

The Right to Education Act provides free education for children aged 6-14.

Public Health and Nutrition

The state should work to improve public health and nutrition levels.

Example:

The National Health Mission focuses on maternal and child health.

Environmental Protection

The government should protect and conserve the environment and natural resources.

Example:

The Forest Conservation Act helps in preserving India's forests.
